## Background and terminology

In the SAP-1 clock circuit, a **bistable** 555 timer acts as a debounced switch, enabling Ben Eater to alternate between an **astable** 555 timer and a **monostable** 555 timer. The astable 555 provides the normal clock signal (whose frequency is tunable with a potentiometer), while the monostable 555 provides a debounced button for single pulse stepping through clock ticks.

The F8-BB clock circuit features a programmatically controllable clock frequency, built from a multiplexer and binary counter splitting the 555 frequency by powers of 2. Fadil-1's permanent clock circuit's maximum frequency is in the 1--2 MHz range, near the limit imposed by the collective propagation delays throughout the rest of the computer.

Since I intend my build to be more of a learning tool, I wanted to retain the transparency afforded by the SAP-1's clock circuit. In particular, I wanted to incorporate a potentiometer to control clock frequency as well as stepwise control from a monostable 555. However, I also added a crystal oscillator as an option for "high-frequency" (>1 kHz) operation.

## Logic overview

As with the F8-BB, I also opted to use all NAND gates to cut down on the number of ICs. The "CLK MODE" switch connected to the bistable 555 timer controls the two modes of operation:

- **RUN** mode: `CLK` is determined by the astable 555 timer, whose frequency is tunable using the potentiometer.
- **STEP** mode: `CLK` is determined by one of two inputs:
  - "XTAL ENABLE" **HIGH**: Crystal oscillator is active and controls `CLK`. *Note*: In this state, the "STEP" button is still connected to the circuit and should not be pressed to avoid unwanted clock ticks.
  - "XTAL ENABLE" **LOW**: Crystal oscillator output is in a high-impedance state and pulled up through a 1 kΩ resistor to +5V. `CLK` is controlled by the "STEP" button connected to the monostable 555 timer.
